The Basics

If you want to learn more about what goes into computers--whether it be for the sake of building a custom desktop, being more informed as a consumer and therefore making a better purchase of a “pre-build,” or desktop sold by a company that is already completely assembled, or you are a technical specialist wanting to refresh your knowledge or learn more about the newest topic--it will first be beneficial to identify what PART of a computer you want to learn about.

We will first cover the basic components of an average computer, what they are, what functions they perform, and then will provide some more specific technical knowledge into how each component works.

Many Parts

Computers are made up of several fundamental parts. They are present in almost all computers. A good starting point is a component called the motherboard. Every computer has a motherboard. This is essentially a large circuit board that enables all of the other components to connect and talk to each other. (Research and provide more information.)

CPUs

The next component to consider is the central processing unit, or CPU. This is one of the most important parts of your computer. You've likely already heard that acronym at some point before. It does exactly what it's name would suggest: it processes the vast majority of information that contributes directly to whatever you're doing on your computer. (Research and provide more info.)

GPUs

Another similar component is the graphics processing unit, or GPU for short. This is similar, although it is responsible for rendering individual pixels, like in videos or video games. Not all computers have a GPU, but they are often built into the motherboard. But things like gaming or other taxing processes will certainly benefit greatly from a GPU! (Research and provide more information.)

Storage

We can next explore various storage types. The first will be a simple drive. This stores information. These are usually hard drives or solid state drives. (Research and provide more information.)
