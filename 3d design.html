3D Design

Intro to 3D Design

3D design is an intricate and extremely technical aspect of post-production in media. It requires great proficiency and a highly specialized skill set. Thankfully, these skills can be learned, and there is a wealth of information available on the subject.

Many Components

The first idea that one must have about 3D design is that 3D design has many facets to it. It is not one practice or study, but multiple. The most fundamental of these and perhaps the first step in any 3D design process is the acquisition of or creation of a model. This is usually referred to simply as 3D modeling.

3D Modeling

3D modeling involves either purchasing a model from a website, a freelancer, a company, etc. In fact, even some movies or franchises will sell the rights to their models! Many more freelancers and individual content creators will create replicas of these models, and sometimes with extreme detail and effort, resulting in a product that may be even more hyper realistic than what was created for the original artwork.

Creating Models

It is also possible to create your own 3D models. Most models work around providing simple geometric shapes that can then be contorted, altered, and shaped to create models like people, animals, characters, etc. Most models you see in movies and television actually started in a simple program from shapes like cubes and spheres!

3D Animation

The next component of 3D design, perhaps most commonly known, is animation. This involves using some 3D software (which we will get into later) to program the movement of your 3D model. This is critical in media types like video game design and modern cinema. Animation can be applied to biological models like humans and animals, it can also be applied to machines and other 3D created objects (a good example is the Transformers series), or it can even be applied to simple 3D text seen in a movie trailer. Animation is just as vital to the process as modeling.
